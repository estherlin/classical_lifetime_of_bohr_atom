Classical Lifetime of the Bohr Atom

View Source
Tutorial

In the Bohr (planetary) model of the hydrogen atom, the electron is assumed to rotate about a circular orbit of fixed radius 0.5 Angstroms about the proton. However, a classical analysis of this model shows this to be false.

There is energy loss from the electric dipole radiation, described by the Larmor formula. Over time, the electron will spiral into the proton due to this energy loss. This fall time of the electron is predicted to be 16 picoseconds.
